## Missing Requirements
- The SRS omits the explicit launch timing from the stakeholder input: “for pilot launch in September.” This appears in the Purpose, but not as a requirement or constraint.
- The transcript says “Patients should log in using the national health identity account only.” FR-1 captures the login method, but the SRS does not state that this is the only permitted identity provider in a way that can be validated with an authentication constraint and exception handling.
- The stakeholder request “Notifications for confirmations/cancellations should be delivered within 10 minutes” is split into FR-12 and FR-13, but the SRS does not specify the notification channels or what counts as “delivered.”
- “Admin dashboard should show daily appointment volume and no-show rate” is captured, but the SRS does not specify whether these metrics are real-time, end-of-day, or filterable by date range.
- “The platform must sync appointment status updates to the hospital EHR system” is included, but the SRS does not define which status updates, when they sync, or whether retries/failure handling are required.
- “During network outages, emergency triage and patient lookup must still operate offline for continuity of care” is only partially reflected. The SRS does not define the minimum offline triage/lookup capabilities needed to satisfy continuity of care.

## Unresolved Contradictions
- The SRS correctly identifies a direct conflict between “During network outages, emergency triage and patient lookup must still operate offline for continuity of care.” and “All user sessions must be validated against the central cloud IAM in real time. Offline access is not allowed for patient data screens.” However, it leaves the contradiction unresolved in the requirements themselves. These statements cannot both be true without a defined offline-authentication exception or a degraded-mode design.
- The SRS correctly flags the retention conflict between “Keep immutable clinical audit logs for 7 years for legal and quality audits.” and “Delete patient interaction logs after 30 days to minimize retention risk.” But it does not resolve whether patient interaction logs are separate from clinical audit logs or whether certain fields are excluded from deletion. This is a material ambiguity, not just an open question.
- FR-17/NFR-4 repeat the 7-year audit log requirement, but the SRS does not clarify whether “patient interaction logs” are part of the immutable audit log set. The transcript’s “Do not change the 7-year immutable audit logging requirement.” means the 30-day deletion memo cannot override it without a defined data-classification rule.

## Hallucinated Requirements
- The SRS adds “admin users” as a stakeholder role in Section 3, but the transcript only says “Roles: patient, clinician, admin.” That role exists, but “admin users” is a wording expansion not present in the source. Minor, but still a rewrite rather than a direct extract.
- FR-2 says “support the following roles: patient, clinician, and admin.” The transcript states “Roles: patient, clinician, admin.” This is fine, but the SRS later implies these are system access roles without any evidence about permissions, which may overstate scope.
- The SRS introduces “continuity-of-care needs” in the Scope section. The transcript mentions “continuity of care” only in the emergency preparedness note, not as a general scope area.
- Section 6 labels items as “Contradictions and Risks,” but the transcript never mentions a “risk” framework or a requirement to maintain such a section. This is process content, not stakeholder content.
- The open question “What is the exact definition of ‘critical pages’ for the 2.5 seconds p95 performance target?” is reasonable analysis, but the SRS then effectively treats “critical pages” as an implementation requirement without source definition. The term appears in the transcript, but not the precise scope of pages.
- NFR-6 duplicates FR-20 (“User sessions shall be validated against the central cloud IAM in real time.”) but the transcript only states this once. The duplication itself is not a hallucination, but it creates an impression of multiple independent requirements where there is only one.
- The SRS states in NFR-4 that audit logs must be retained “for legal and quality audits.” The transcript says “for legal and quality audits,” so that part is accurate; however, the SRS elevates this into a standalone non-functional requirement alongside FR-17, creating redundancy and potential over-specification.

## Overall Assessment
NEEDS REVISION. The SRS captures many core stakeholder needs, but it does not resolve the key contradiction between offline continuity-of-care and real-time IAM validation/no offline access, and it leaves the audit-log retention conflict ambiguous. It also omits or under-defines several important operational details such as notification channels, EHR sync behavior, and the exact offline capabilities required.
