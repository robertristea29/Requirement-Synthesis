## Missing Requirements
- The SRS does not capture the September pilot launch constraint with enough operational detail. The transcript says, "We need a Hospital Appointment and Triage Coordination System for pilot launch in September," but the SRS only repeats this in FR-1 without any acceptance or readiness criteria for a pilot release.
- The requirement that "Notifications for confirmations/cancellations should be delivered within 10 minutes" is included, but the SRS omits the full notification scope. The transcript names both confirmations and cancellations together; the SRS splits them into FR-13 and FR-14 but does not state whether both notification types are required for all appointment actions or what channel(s) are used.
- The transcript states, "Clinicians need to create and update triage records with severity, symptoms, and priority." The SRS lists the fields, but it does not define any workflow, validation, edit permissions, or required sequence for triage record creation and update.
- The transcript says, "The platform must sync appointment status updates to the hospital EHR system." The SRS includes this, but it is missing timing, frequency, failure handling, and which statuses are in scope.
- The transcript requires, "Admin dashboard should show daily appointment volume and no-show rate." The SRS includes these metrics, but it does not define whether the dashboard needs filters, date ranges, export, or refresh behavior.
- The emergency preparedness requirement says, "During network outages, emergency triage and patient lookup must still operate offline for continuity of care." The SRS states offline operation for these functions, but it does not define what "offline" means operationally, how data sync is restored, or any degraded-mode rules.
- The security architecture note says, "All user sessions must be validated against the central cloud IAM in real time." The SRS includes the requirement, but it does not specify authentication session timeout, token refresh, or what happens if IAM is unavailable.
- The transcript and compliance notes together imply two distinct record types: "Keep immutable clinical audit logs for 7 years for legal and quality audits" and "Delete patient interaction logs after 30 days to minimize retention risk." The SRS identifies the conflict, but it does not define what records belong to each category.

## Unresolved Contradictions
- The SRS correctly identifies a direct conflict, but it does not resolve it: "During network outages, emergency triage and patient lookup must still operate offline for continuity of care" versus "Offline access is not allowed for patient data screens." These requirements are mutually incompatible unless "patient data screens" is narrowly defined, which the SRS does not do.
- The retention requirements remain contradictory and unresolved: "Keep immutable clinical audit logs for 7 years for legal and quality audits" versus "Delete patient interaction logs after 30 days to minimize retention risk." The SRS asks an open question, but the specification itself still contains both requirements without a rule for separating log types or prioritizing compliance.
- FR-18 and NFR-6 are duplicates in substance: "The system shall keep immutable clinical audit logs for 7 years for legal and quality audits" and "Clinical audit logs shall be immutable and retained for 7 years." This is not a logical contradiction, but it is an internal inconsistency in specification structure because the same requirement appears twice with different wording.
- FR-21 and NFR-4 duplicate the same requirement: "All user sessions shall be validated against the central cloud IAM in real time." Again, not contradictory, but this creates ambiguity about whether the requirement is functional, non-functional, or both.

## Hallucinated Requirements
- The SRS introduces "security controls" in the Scope section as a separate scope item, but the transcript does not explicitly request a generic security-controls capability beyond session validation and offline restriction.
- FR-1 turns the overall product description into a requirement: "The system shall support a Hospital Appointment and Triage Coordination System for pilot launch in September." This is a paraphrase of the purpose statement, not a testable stakeholder requirement.
- The "Contradictions and Risks" section and the "Open Questions" section are analyst-created content and not stakeholder requirements. These are acceptable as analysis artifacts, but they should not be mistaken for sourced requirements.
- NFR-6 appears to restate FR-18 with a different label and could be seen as a synthesized requirement not explicitly stated as a separate need in the transcript.

## Overall Assessment
NEEDS REVISION. The SRS captures many core stakeholder needs, but it leaves several important requirements underspecified and does not resolve two major contradictions: offline emergency access versus no offline patient data screens, and 7-year audit retention versus 30-day deletion of patient interaction logs. It also contains duplicated requirements and some analyst-generated wording that is not clearly traceable to the transcript.
